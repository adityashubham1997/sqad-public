## Layer 2 — Deep Contrarian Analysis

### Anti-Fragility Scoring
Taleb's framework: does this company BENEFIT from volatility (anti-fragile), resist it
(robust), or break (fragile)? Evidence-based classification — not vibes.

### Information Asymmetry Detection
Who knows more? Where is information asymmetry highest?
Signs: insider activity diverging from public statements, credit ≠ equity, options pricing
in events equity market ignores.

### Behavioral Bias Checklist
Systematically check: confirmation bias, anchoring, availability bias, survivorship bias,
narrative fallacy, authority bias, recency bias, loss aversion, sunk cost.

### Reflexivity Analysis (Soros)
Market prices don't just reflect reality — they CHANGE it. Map reflexive loops:
high stock price → cheap equity → acquisitions → justifies high price.
Which reflexive loops are currently active?

### Narrative Economics Assessment
What narrative is the market telling? Stage: emerging / consensus / crowded / breaking.
Crowded narratives are the most dangerous.

## Layer 3 — Quant-Grade Adversarial Epistemics

### Calibrated Probability Forecasting
Every prediction = calibrated probability. Enforce equivalent bet test:
"Would you bet $700 to win $300 on this? If not, your 70% is too high."
Check calibration: of claims rated 70% confident, 70% should actually be true.
Academic basis: Tetlock (2015) "Superforecasting"

### Reference Class Forecasting
For every prediction: identify the appropriate REFERENCE CLASS first.
Apply outside view (base rate) BEFORE inside view (company-specific analysis).
Only adjust away from base rate with SPECIFIC, QUANTIFIED evidence.
Academic basis: Kahneman & Tversky (1979), Flyvbjerg (2006)

### Adversarial Red Team Protocol
Formally assume the role of someone SHORTING this thesis:
- 3 strongest arguments against (must be as well-evidenced as the bull case)
- Specific data that would DISPROVE the thesis
- Timeline: "If [X] doesn't happen by [date], thesis is dead"
- Kill shot: the single piece of information that flips all agents
Academic basis: Klein (2007)

### Falsifiability Certification
Every major conclusion must pass Popper's test: "What specific, observable evidence
would prove this WRONG?" 
Classify: FALSIFIABLE / PARTIALLY FALSIFIABLE / UNFALSIFIABLE (= narrative, not prediction)
Academic basis: Popper (1959)

### Fermi Estimation Cross-Check
Cross-check every major quantitative claim with an independent Fermi estimate.
If detailed model says X but Fermi says Y and they differ by >3x → flag for investigation.
"Model says TAM $500B. Fermi: [population] × [adoption%] × [spend] = $50B. 10x discrepancy → investigate."
Academic basis: Douglas Hubbard (2010)

### Coherence Audit (Dutch Book Test)
Verify combined probability assignments across all agents are internally consistent.
No Dutch Book (guaranteed loss). Check: (a) total probability > 100%? (overconfidence)
(b) P(A and B) > P(A)? (logical error) (c) scenarios sum < 100%? (missing scenario)
Academic basis: De Finetti (1937)

### Superforecasting Protocol (Tetlock's Method)
1. Start with base rate (outside view)
2. Adjust for specific evidence (inside view)
3. Weight adjustments by evidence quality
4. Update incrementally
5. Track prediction accuracy over time for calibration
